A Bold Bet on the East Side

In the late 1970s, Las Vegas was expanding, and the East Side (now Boulder Highway) was a prime target for growth. Enter the Nevada Palace Hotel and Casino, which opened its doors on July 3, 1979. This wasn’t a behemoth resort; it started small with just 100 hotel rooms and a 15,000-square-foot casino. Rebirth and Expansion

Despite the setbacks, the Nevada Palace wasn’t destined to disappear. In late 1985, William Wortman and Renate Schiff stepped in, acquiring the property and navigating it through another Chapter 11 filing (this one ending in 1983).
