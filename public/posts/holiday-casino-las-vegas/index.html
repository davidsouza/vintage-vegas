A Steamboat on the Strip: The Birth of the Holiday Casino

The Las Vegas Strip has seen countless transformations, but few stories are as charmingly unique as that of the Holiday Casino. Opened on July 2, 1973, it wasn’t just another casino; it was a bold attempt to capture the romance of the Mississippi River in the heart of the desert. Initially planned as the ‘River Queen,’ the casino, developed by Holiday Queen Land Corporation in partnership with Holiday Inn, offered a refreshing alternative to the increasingly opulent and exclusive casinos that were becoming the norm. Shelby and Claudine Williams, owners of the Silver Slipper, envisioned a place for everyday gamblers, and their vision paid off handsomely. The adjacent Holiday Inn Center Strip hotel, opening in 1972, laid the groundwork, creating a convenient and accessible destination.
