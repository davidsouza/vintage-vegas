Remembering the Hotel San Remo: A Slice of Italy on the Las Vegas Strip

A Different Kind of Vegas Vibe

The late 1980s and early 1990s were a period of dramatic transformation for the Las Vegas Strip. Mega-resorts were springing up, vying for attention with dazzling lights and extravagant themes. But nestled just east of the main drag, at 115 East Tropicana Avenue, was something a little different: the Hotel San Remo. Embracing the Mediterranean Spirit

What made the San Remo special wasn’t sheer size, but its unique atmosphere. While giants like the Luxor and Excalibur were captivating visitors with their grandeur, the San Remo cultivated a feeling of intimacy and personalized service. As the late 1990s and early 2000s rolled around, the relentless expansion of megaresorts began to take its toll. The San Remo struggled to maintain its niche in a market dominated by colossal properties with seemingly limitless budgets. Despite attempts at renovation, the financial pressures mounted. The inevitable occurred in 2006 when the Hotel San Remo officially closed its doors, giving way to the Hooters Casino Hotel.
