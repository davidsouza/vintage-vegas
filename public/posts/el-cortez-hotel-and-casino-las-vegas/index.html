A Pioneer on Fremont: The El Cortez Opens Its Doors

The year was 1941. The world was on the brink of war, but in Las Vegas, a different kind of excitement was brewing. On November 7th, the El Cortez Hotel and Casino officially opened its doors, joining a fledgling collection of casinos along Fremont Street. Originally owned by Jim Cortese (hence the name “El Cortez”), the hotel offered a modest 60 rooms, a crucial difference from the rapidly expanding establishments further down the nascent Las Vegas Strip. The El Cortez remained a family-run operation for decades, fostering a sense of loyalty among both employees and patrons.
