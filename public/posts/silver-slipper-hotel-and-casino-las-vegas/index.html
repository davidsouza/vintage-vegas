Remembering the Silver Slipper: A Vintage Las Vegas Strip Gem

A Glimmer of Silver on the Strip

For those of us who remember the ‘glory days’ of the Las Vegas Strip, the Silver Slipper Hotel and Casino holds a special place in our memories. Opened on September 1, 1950, in Winchester, Nevada (which would soon become part of Las Vegas), the Silver Slipper was more than just another casino; it was a distinctive landmark positioned strategically near the bustling Frontier Hotel and Casino. Interestingly, both properties shared the same ownership initially, creating a symbiotic relationship that contributed to their success.

The Rotating Slipper ‚Äì A Neon Icon

What truly set the Silver Slipper apart was its magnificent, 12-foot-tall neon slipper. This rotating spectacle boasted over 900 bulbs and was visible for miles along North Las Vegas Boulevard. It was the photo opportunity for tourists, and a vital part of the Strip‚Äôs visual identity during the 1950s, 60s, and 70s. Imagine the sight ‚Äì a giant, gleaming slipper spinning against the desert night sky! That iconic slipper, thankfully, wasn’t lost to time. It now resides at the Las Vegas Neon Museum, where it continues to dazzle and remind us of the Strip’s vibrant past.

A High-Profile Tenant: Howard Hughes

The Silver Slipper‚Äôs story takes another intriguing turn in 1968 when it caught the eye of Howard Hughes. He leased the casino, a significant event demonstrating the Strip’s growing allure and importance. Hughes’ presence further elevated the Silver Slipper’s profile, though the gaming operations were later leased out separately. Hughes maintained the lease until his death in 1976, a testament to his continued interest in the evolving Las Vegas landscape.

A Final Curtain Call and a Lost Dream

Sadly, the Silver Slipper’s time on the Strip came to an end on November 28, 1988. The closure marked the loss of another piece of vintage Vegas. The property was demolished to make way for a much-needed parking lot for the Frontier, a pragmatic but ultimately heartbreaking decision for those who cherished the Silver Slipper’s unique character. Numerous plans were floated over the years to rebuild the Silver Slipper Hotel and Casino, fueled by nostalgia and the desire to recapture a piece of old Vegas. However, these dreams never materialized.

A Vacant Lot & A Lasting Legacy

Today, the site of the Silver Slipper, directly across from the opulent Encore Hotel Casino, remains vacant. While new developments continue to shape the Las Vegas Strip, the memory of the Silver Slipper lives on, a reminder of a simpler, more whimsical era in Las Vegas history. It serves as a poignant symbol of the constant evolution ‚Äì and sometimes, the heartbreaking loss ‚Äì of the city‚Äôs vibrant identity.
