## A Vision Takes Root (and Almost Doesn't)

The story of the International Hotel is more than just about a building; it's a tale of ambition, rivalry, and the relentless expansion of Las Vegas. The land, originally part of the Las Vegas Park racetrack, sat far enough east of the Strip in the 1960s that it was considered a risky location. In 1965, Marvin Kratter, of National Equities, first envisioned a massive resort complex, complete with a 1,500-room hotel, a golf course, and private residences. However, Kratter's vision remained unrealized.

Enter Kirk Kerkorian, the aviation mogul and a man known for his audacious bets. In 1967, he swooped in and purchased a substantial 65.5-acre portion of Kratterâ€™s land for $5 million.  Kerkorian's plan was even grander: a 1,510-room, 30-story hotel costing $50 million. ## The Hughes Factor & a Race to the Finish

Kerkorian wasn't building in a vacuum. Howard Hughes, already a significant player in Las Vegas with his [Sands](/posts/sands-hotel-and-casino-las-vegas/) Hotel, saw Kerkorian as a direct competitor. Hughes launched a series of attempts to derail the International's construction. These included expanding his own Sands to challenge its scale, spreading rumors about nuclear test site damage potentially impacting the area, and even offering to buy the property outright. All failed. 

The rivalry reached a fever pitch.  Hughes, unable to stop the International, accelerated the construction of the unfinished [Landmark Hotel and Casino](/posts/landmark-hotel-and-casino-las-vegas/) directly across Paradise Road.  The Landmark opened a single day *before* the International, a petty victory in a fierce battle.

## An Era of Entertainment & Elvis

Despite the drama, the International Hotel officially opened on July 2, 1969.  At 30 stories, it was instantly the tallest building in Nevada and the state's largest hotel. Entertainment director Bill Miller quickly secured star power, signing the likes of Barbra Streisand and Peggy Lee. But the biggest coup was yet to come: Elvis Presley. The King of Rock 'n' Roll began his groundbreaking seven-year run on July 31, 1969, performing an astonishing 636 consecutive sold-out shows.
