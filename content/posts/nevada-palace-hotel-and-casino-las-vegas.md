## A Bold Bet on the East Side

In the late 1970s, Las Vegas was expanding, and the East Side (now Boulder Highway) was a prime target for growth. Enter the Nevada Palace Hotel and Casino, which opened its doors on July 3, 1979. This wasn't a behemoth resort; it started small with just 100 hotel rooms and a 15,000-square-foot casino. The initial optimism proved fleeting. Just a year after opening, Gene Lucas departed, and the Nevada Palace plunged into financial turmoil. A Chapter 11 bankruptcy filing followed swiftly.
