## Embracing the Irish Spirit

The real transformation came in 1987 when Reno-based Lincoln Management Group, operating as Fitzgeralds Gaming, purchased the Sundance. They made a bold move, rebranding it as [Fitzgeralds Las Vegas](/posts/fitzgeralds-las-vegas/) and imbuing it with a distinctly Irish identity. Suddenly, shamrocks sprouted everywhere, green trim adorned the exterior, and a cheerful cartoon leprechaun became the casino's mascot. This playful, value-oriented branding was a deliberate strategy, offering a contrast to the increasingly high-roller focused Las Vegas Strip. Fitzgeralds became known for its accessible gaming, lively atmosphere, and affordability.  It was *the* place to be for a fun, budget-friendly Vegas experience. The casino floor offered a 42,000-square-foot playground of slots, table games, keno, and a popular sportsbook, and the restaurants, particularly the buffet and steakhouse, were always buzzing.

## The D & A Lasting Legacy

In 2011, brothers Derek and Greg Stevens, already owners of the [Golden Gate](/posts/golden-gate-hotel-and-casino-las-vegas/), acquired Fitzgeralds. They initiated a $22 million renovation project, modernizing the casino and guest rooms while retaining the original tower. In 2012, the property officially became The D Las Vegas, marking the end of an era for Fitzgeralds. While the vibrant green and playful leprechauns are long gone, the bones of Fitzgeralds live on. The D's casino and structure are a direct continuation of the original property.
