## The Strike That Stilled the Doors

The late 1980s were a turbulent time for Las Vegas. A major hotel and casino strike began in 1984, significantly impacting the downtown area. The Holiday International, then operating under the Holiday Inn banner, was among the properties shuttered. The strike lasted for three long years, paralyzing operations and causing considerable financial strain for the owners and employees alike.  This prolonged closure left a visible mark on the downtown landscape, a stark reminder of the contentious relationship between casino owners and the Culinary Union.  The impact extended beyond just the immediate business; it cast a shadow over the perception of downtown Las Vegas, contributing to the shift of focus towards the burgeoning Las Vegas Strip.
