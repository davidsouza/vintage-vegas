## A Desert Oasis: The Casbah's Early Days

The mid-1960s were a vibrant time for Las Vegas. The Rat Pack reigned supreme, neon signs pulsed with energy, and the Las Vegas Strip was constantly evolving. Amidst this backdrop, the Casbah Hotel and Casino sprang to life in 1963. Built by the ambitious Danny Jackson, the casino leaned into an exotic Moroccan theme. Think intricately patterned carpets, lush greenery, and a decidedly different atmosphere than the more overtly Western-themed casinos of the era. It offered a taste of the mysterious and romantic, catering to a clientele seeking something a little different. Jacksonâ€™s family continued to operate the casino after his death, but it struggled to establish a truly clean reputation.

## A Reputation and a Reckoning

Sadly, the Casbah wasn't known solely for its decor. Like several Vegas establishments of the time, it battled a persistent undercurrent of problems: prostitution and gang-related violence. This tarnished its image, impacting business and contributing to a sense of unease. It was a common, albeit undesirable, aspect of Vegas history that many casinos had to confront. The Casbah, unfortunately, never quite shook it.

However, the underlying problems proved difficult to completely eradicate. The shadow of its past lingered.

## Demise and Legacy

Despite Meyers' efforts, the Queen of Hearts (formerly the Casbah) ultimately couldn't escape its fate. In 2010, the announcement came: the hotel would be demolished. The land, strategically located on the Strip, was slated for a new Las Vegas City Hall building and accompanying business complex.  The demolition marked the end of an era. 

While the Casbah/Queen of Hearts might be gone, its story remains a fascinating chapter in vintage Las Vegas history. It represents a complex mix of ambition, cultural trends, the enduring challenges of running a Vegas casino, and the resilience of a woman breaking barriers in a male-dominated industry. The site now serves a different purpose, but echoes of the Moroccan mystique and the pioneering spirit of Ann Meyers linger in the memories of those who remember the Casbah's unique place on the Las Vegas Strip.
