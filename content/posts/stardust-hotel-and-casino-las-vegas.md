## A Star is Born: The Stardust's Grand Entrance

The Las Vegas Strip wouldn't be what it is today without the Stardust Hotel and Casino. Opened on July 2, 1958, it was a behemoth. Imagine: 1,065 rooms, a sprawling 16,500 square foot casino, and a whopping 60 acres of land! It was, quite simply, the largest hotel in the world at the time. The story began with Tony Cornero, who initially spearheaded the project. Following his untimely death in 1955, his brother took over until Rella Factor finalized the deal and brought the Stardust to life. ## The '50s and '60s: Glamour, Country Clubs, and a Speedway

The Stardust quickly became synonymous with the glamorous Rat Pack era. It hosted iconic performers and attracted a sophisticated clientele. But the Stardust was more than just a casino and hotel. During the 1960s, it was an experience. The resort operated a private country club, catering to high rollers, and even had its own raceway, a testament to the ambitious scope of the ownership. Adding the Royal Nevada hotel-casino in 1959 further expanded its footprint. A nine-story addition in 1964 just proved its continued popularity and growth.

## A Final Chapter: Demise and Legacy

The Boyd family acquired the Stardust in 1985, and though they maintained the property for two decades, competition from newer, flashier resorts began to take its toll. By 2006, despite boasting an 85,000 square foot casino and 1,552 rooms, the Stardust struggled to remain competitive.
