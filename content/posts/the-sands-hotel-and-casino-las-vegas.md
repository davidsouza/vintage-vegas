## The Mob Connection & The Rat Pack Era

The Sands' trajectory took a significant turn when figures like Meyer Lansky and Doc Stacher acquired shares in the operation. While this association adds a layer of intrigue to the hotel's history, it also proved to be a catalyst for its future fame. ## Silver Screen & Howard Hughes' Acquisition

The Sands' fame continued to grow with appearances in popular culture. Then, in 1967, the Sands became the property of Howard Hughes, a man who seemed to embody the spirit of Las Vegas itself. Hughes invested heavily, adding a substantial 500-room tower, expanding the hotel's capacity and reinforcing its position on the Strip. 

## Decline & Demise: Making Way for Venice

Despite its storied past and continued popularity, the Sands began to face increasing competition in the late 1980s. Newer, lavish resorts began to dominate the landscape. In 1989, Sheldon Adelson and partners acquired the Sands, hoping to revitalize the property. They introduced the Sands Expo and Convention Center, a significant shift intended to attract convention business and broaden Las Vegas' appeal.  However, these efforts proved insufficient. Ultimately, in 1996, Adelson made the difficult decision to demolish the Sands to make way for his ambitious vision: The Venetian, a replica of Venice, Italy, costing $1.5 billion. The implosion on November 26, 1996, marked the end of an era, the final curtain call for a beloved Las Vegas landmark. Though gone, the legend of The Sands lives on, forever etched in the history of the Las Vegas Strip.
