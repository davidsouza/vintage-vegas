## A Fremont Street Favorite: The Derby Turf Club Emerges

The Las Vegas Strip's evolution is fascinating, and often, the stories are not found *on* the Strip itself, but just a few blocks away. Take the Derby Turf Club, for example. Founded in December 1953, it wasn't a sprawling resort or a glitzy showplace, but a dedicated race and sports book located at 113 South 1st Street, nestled just off the bustling Fremont Street experience. Initially co-owned by Louis Nier and David Stearns, the Derby Turf Club quickly established itself as a hub for serious horse racing enthusiasts in a city rapidly embracing gambling.  Its focus was laser-sharp: racing. While many Vegas casinos were expanding into other forms of wagering, the Derby Turf Club specialized, catering to a clientele eager for detailed information and competitive odds on races from around the country.
