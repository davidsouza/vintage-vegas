## From Restaurant to Vegas Landmark: The Pioneer's Early Years

The corner of First and Fremont Street in Las Vegas holds a lot of history, and the building currently housing a souvenir shop is no exception. Long before the mega-resorts and dazzling lights, a simple restaurant stood there, dating back to 1918. ## The [Elwell](/posts/elwell-hotel-and-casino-las-vegas/) Acquisition & A Hotel is Born

What truly set the Pioneer apart was its unique approach. It wasn't just a gambling den; it was a social hub. The club's owners understood the changing landscape and sought expansion. In 1965, a significant move was made: the purchase of The Elwell Hotel. This acquisition allowed the Pioneer Club to become the Pioneer Club Hotel, significantly expanding its footprint and appealing to a wider clientele. ## A Short-Lived Hotel, A Lasting Image

## Demise and Legacy: Vegas Vic and the Pioneer Spirit

The relentless growth of Las Vegas and the rise of larger, more opulent casinos ultimately took their toll. In 1984, the Pioneer Club was sold to the Golden Nugget, who, in a move that saddened many longtime Vegas enthusiasts, demolished the hotel portion to build a parking garage. The club itself finally closed its doors in 1995, unable to compete with the behemoths that now dominated the Strip.
